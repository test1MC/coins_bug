Subcontractor Maintenance - Verification Tab

The Verification tab enables you to input the details of the CIS verification with the Inland Revenue. All Subcontractors will have some form of code that they will have, even if they are not registered they will have “New” or be exempt. 

    Select the Verification tab to enter the information for the Subcontractor relating to their status for CIS. 

Fields

Description

Business Type:

The type of business, i.e. company, or partnership.

Declaration of Engagement:

This relates to you as a company. Selecting the Tick box is declaring that you have placed a contract of work with the Subcontractor.

Trading Name:

The exact trading name that is registered with the HMRC.

Partnership Name:

The Name of the Partnership.

Subcontract UTR:

The Subcontractor’s Unique Taxpayer Reference Number.

Partnership UTR:

The Partner’s Unique Taxpayer Reference Number.

Company Reg:

The Subcontractor’s Company Registration Number.

NI Number:

The subcontractor’s National Insurance Number.

First Name:

Second Name:

Surname:

The given name of the main Partner or individual.

The Family Name

Verify Address:

Post Code:

The Address and Post Code that will be used for verification purposes.

HMRC Notified:

This subcontractor has already been registered with the HMRC. The HMRC has notified you of the details, or notified that the details have changed.

Verification Status:

The Status of the Subcontractor.

Tax Treatment:

The Subcontractor’s tax treatment as supplied by HMRC, which is automatically updated.

Verification date:

The date on which the Subcontractor was verified.

Verification Number:

This number is supplied by HMRC when the Subcontractor has been verified.

The verification Status of the Subcontractor can be any one of the following -

  • New - This is a new record that has not been registered with the HMRC.
  • Submitted - a verification request has been submitted but nothing has been received from the HMRC
  • Failed - the details submitted did not match the details held by the HMRC. (You will not be able to pay the Subcontractor until these have been verified.
  • Unmatched - HMRC has issued a unique verification number to make payments at the higher rate of deduction
  • Matched - the details match and you can pay the subcontractor. The Tax Treatment field shows if the payment is gross or net.
  • Transition - if the record is in transition.

Enter the details in the relevant fields and select the Contacts Tab
