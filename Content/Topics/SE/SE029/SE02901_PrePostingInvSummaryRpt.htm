Pre-Posting Invoice Summary Report

The Pre-Posting Invoice Summary Report allows management to review invoices before printing/posting. For this report, the Cost Basis for Billing is used for each Billing Detail line, allowing for Nominal Gross Margin (GM) calculations for the invoice only. Gross Billings TD, as well as EMAC, are shown to assist in an overall review of the . The Cost Basis for Billing is the ‘cost’ value assigned to the Billing Detail Line.

  • For that has not been posted via payroll, the report uses the 's Cost Class Rate so that the estimated gross margin may be shown on the report. When PR posts and the report is rerun, the true incurred costs are shown and used for calculations.
  • For non- items, the Cost field on the billing detail line is used. This may be a committed cost from a Purchase Order, an incurred cost from , or a cost amount manually entered by a user to calculate the billing line.
  • For Add-ons, the Cost field is ignored in the report calculations as this would cause the gross margin calculations to be inaccurate.

Sample Report

After you run the Print Pre-Posting Invoice Summary action, the Pre-Posting Invoice Summary Report that is generated (Figure 3: Sample Pre-Posting Invoice Summary Report) includes information for only the Invoices in the selected batch. Although this report is similar to the Invoice Summary Report discussed later in this document, it differs in the following ways:

  • The report includes unposted invoices.
  • The last 4 columns are different.
  • A Cost Basis row is shown to help verify the Nominal GM values.

Sample Pre-Posting Invoice Summary Report

Other facts about this report include:

  • Each Billing Detail Line has a Cost Basis for Billing. For Detail Lines generated from cost, this is the true cost. For other Detail Lines, the Cost value can be directly entered.
  • Nominal Gross Margin = Sum of Invoice Line Bill Amounts – Sum of Cost Basis for Billing for the Invoice Lines
  • Gross Billings TD is the sum of all posted billings to date for the , including tax.
  • Nominal Gross Margin, Nominal Gross Margin %, and EMAC are suppressed for Scheduled Billing invoices since the has no costs.
