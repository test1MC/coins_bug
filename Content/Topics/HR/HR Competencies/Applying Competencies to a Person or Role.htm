Applying Competencies to a Person or Role

 

Competencies are usually inherited by an individual because it is assigned to their ‘job title’. Competencies can be assigned to a job title and can be a mixture of skill sets or even health and safety requirements.

Job titles are in turn assigned to ‘Job Groups’. This builds flexibility to duplicate job titles/roles into different Job Groups and assign to employees.

 

For example a different set of competencies may apply to the same job title role but in different parts of an organisation.

 

also has the flexibility to apply competencies specifically to an individual, irrespective of their ‘normal’ competencies on their job title.

 

For example a competency may be set up for ‘Advanced First Aid’ which is not specific to any formal job role.

 

Alternatively an individual may have 10 competencies that normally apply to their job role – but the person is on modified duties and 3 of the 10 no longer apply - and need taking off.
