SHO Changes 2016

Traditionally, sales were mainly ILF/ALF, but increasingly are making different kinds of sales based around a subscription model.

With an ILF/ALF deal, there is a large initial capex which is the initial cost of the software and an annual fee witch must be paid to continue using the software. In the subscription model, there is a single fee. The fee is based on what the customer would have paid in the traditional capex (ILF + ALF), plus an uplift for the cost of money, which is then spread in equal instalments over the period of the contract. This is typically a 3 to 5 year period. Unless cancelled, at the end of the contract this fee then continues until such a time as notice to cancel is received from the customer and or a new contract is negotiated.

It is also common to agree payment plans, which are ILF/ALF agreements, but the cost of the deal is spread equally over a number of years and at the end of the term, the contracts revert to standard ILF/ALF arrangements.

For subscriptions and Payment plans, the new SHO provides for compound interest to be calculated to allow for inflation when costs are spread over a number of years.

The new SHO has been designed to cater for the new types of deals and to provide consistency in the format of the information recorded, for auditing purposes.

The New SHO is divided into several key sections:

  • Header info, including customer name and address
  • ERP Product, by ERP Bundle or by individual component Product – used to record the component parts of ILF or Rental
  • Other Products,
    • ILF/ALF or Rental basis: eCommerce; Mobile
    • ILF/ALF basis: System / data integration; User management; Web Services
    • Rental basis: Managed services (including Hosting); Hardware
  • Third Party Products ILF & ALF
  • Professional Services
  • Financial Summary
  • Invoicing and special contract terms
  • Special Licensing

On the new SHO, boxes in green are free format entry - although some have drop-downs to assist in the completion of the form.

Pink and Blue boxes will allow a degree of typing.

White boxses are restricted and cannot be entered into.

There is a fixed list of services that can be sold. Should you encounter a need to sell something that is not on the list, you will need to commission a new version of the SHO. You must not attemp to overwrite an existing entry in order to add any other product that is not on the SHO.
