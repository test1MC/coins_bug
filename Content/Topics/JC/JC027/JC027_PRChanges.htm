Payroll Changes

The changes that have been made in Payroll to have a dedicated Pay Shift field include:

  • Adding a Pay Shift field for Employee Earnings Categories, Rates, Prevailing Wage Rates, Union Rates, Standard Rates, and Timecards.
  • Adding a Pay Shift field to certain Payroll reports and that include Timecard Transaction detail.
  • Enhancing Employee Self Service (ESS) by adding a Pay Shift field for the Payroll Pay Types and PR Time Card Input File.
  • Updating PR Transfer programs to take Pay Shifts into account.

PR Standard Rate

A new Valid Pay Shifts column has been added to Payroll > Company Setup > Standard Rate, as shown in Figure 7. It contains all non-dormant Pay Shift Codes entered in Pay Shift Codes maintenance. A single code, blank code, and asterisk (*) are also allowed.

  • The blank code can be included on any position of the list, as shown in the examples below.
  • “,1WD,2WD” (blank is in the first position)
  • “1WD,,2WD” (blank is in the second position)
  • “1WD,2WD,” (blank is in the last position)
  • The default value for this field is an asterisk (*).
  • If Pay Shift Codes Maintenance contains no values or all entered codes are dormant, the Pay Shift field is disabled.

Standard Rate Valid Pay Shifts

  • You cannot enter two records for the same Earning Category and Pay Shift with the same priority. For example, you cannot enter 2 different Standard Rate (SLR) records that differ only in rates and pay shifts when the first SLR record has available pay shifts “1WD,2WD,1SA” and the second record has available pay shifts “2WD,1WD,1SU”, because pay shifts 1WD and 2WD will both have the same priority. The same is true for earning categories and for the combination of earning categories and pay shifts.
  • You cannot repeat the pay shifts in the Valid Pay Shifts list for the SLR record. For example, the pay shift list “1WD,,1WD,,2WD” is incorrect because it includes 1WD twice, as well as a blank pay shift twice.
  • If the pay shift list is not an asterisk (*) and is not blank, it must exist and is not dormant.

PR Timesheets Entry

A new Pay Shift column has been added to the Timecard tab of Payroll > [PR frequency] > Input > Timesheets, as shown in Figure 8. It contains all non-dormant Pay Shift Codes entered in Pay Shift Codes maintenance.

  • When a record is added using Timesheets Entry, the Pay Shift field defaults from the previous timecard of the . If it is the first timecard, the Pay Shift is blank.

  • The defaulting of timecard rates is updated by taking the Pay Shift field into account when you enter an Earning Category, Union, Trade, Skill Level, PW Schedule, PW Trade, and PW Skill.

    PR Timecard Rate Defaults

    An example of the setup of an Employee Earning Category is shown in Figure 12. The employee in this example has two Pay Shifts with an HR Earning Category and no Pay Shift for the HO Earning Category.

    A blank Pay Shift can be used when:

    • The Earning Category may only use a blank Pay Shift, meaning that the Pay Shift does not pertain to that type of Earning Category.
    • An exact match is not found for the Pay Shift.

    Employee Earning Categories Setup Example

    The scenarios below demonstrate how Timecard Rates may default in, with only the Employee Earning Category being used (not Union Rates, etc.).

    Scenario #1: If it is the first timecard, the Rate defaults in from the Employee’s Primary Earning Category.

    Earning Category: HR
    Pay Shift: 1WD
    Regular Rate: 31.00 defaults from Employee Primary Earning Category

    Scenario #2: Timecard is entered and Pay Shift is changed to 2WD. The Rate is evaluated and changed accordingly.

    Earning Category: HR
    Pay Shift: 2WD
    Regular Rate: 25.00 defaults from Employee Primary Earning Category

    Scenario #3: Timecard is entered and Pay Shift is changed to 1SA; there is no exact match and no HR/Blank to use. This results in a 0.00 value to be shown.

    Earning Category: HR
    Pay Shift: 1SA
    Regular Rate: 0.00 defaults, no match found, and no blank Pay Shift exists

    Scenario #4: Timecard is entered, Earning Category is changed to HO, and Pay Shift is changed to Blank. The Rate is evaluated and changed accordingly.

    Earning Category: HO
    Pay Shift: Blank
    Regular Rate: 32.00 defaults from Employee Earning Category

    Scenario #5: Timecard is entered, Earning Category is changed to HO, and Pay Shift is changed to 2WD. There is no exact match, but HO/Blank can be used. This results in a rate of 32.00.

    Earning Category: HO
    Pay Shift: 2WD
    Regular Rate: 32.00 defaults from Employee Earning Category
