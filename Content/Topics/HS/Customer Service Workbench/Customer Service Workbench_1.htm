2.1 Customer Service Workbench

Customer Service Workbench helps you to manage your after-sales process.  It provides you with all the relevant information and allows you to log and manage Customer Service calls.

Navigate to the Customer Service Module.

Select Customer Service Workbench.

2.1.1 Contacts Tab

The Contacts tab lists contacts in the House Sales database, including owners, purchasers, and agents.  There is a link to the Customer Service details for the plot on which the contact is recorded; if the contact is recorded on more than one plot, you will first be presented with a list of those plots.

Select the Contacts Tab.

The initial frame in this workbench lists the names, addresses and telephone numbers of all purchasers who have reserved a plot or part-exchange property with you. The list also includes the names and addresses of other contacts associated with a plot, who may not necessarily be the purchaser.

You can select a purchaser from the list to view details of the plot(s) or properties they currently have reserved, or are in the process of purchasing and/or have purchased. You can view, add and amend existing details as required.

You can filter to include/exclude contacts on active or inactive plots by using the Apply Filter button.

The contacts list can be filtered to include/exclude contacts on all or just active or inactive plots. Select the Filter drop-down list and choose Contacts on Active Plots, Contacts on Inactive Plots or Contacts on All Plots from the list and then either press Return or select the Apply Filter button.

To view the plot’s summary details select the surname of one of the contacts attached to the plot.

 

2.1.2 Developments Tab

The developments tab lists the developments you have access to in the House Sales database.  There is a link to the plots on each development; from each plot record you can open the Customer Service plot details.

Select the Developments Tab. 

The development list can be filtered to include/exclude inactive, active and completed developments. Select the Filter drop-down and select Active, Inactive or Complete from the list and then either press Return or select the Apply Filter button.

To view the plot’s summary details select the development number for the site; this will display the development’s plots.

Select the plot code hyperlink to display the plot’s summary details:

 

 

2.1.3 Calls Tab

Customer Service Workbench Calls lists the Customer Service calls.  There is a link from each call to the call details, or to the Customer Service plot details for the plot that the call relates to.

Select the Calls Tab. 

There are also several filter options on the call screen. In the simple filters you can choose to show just open, just closed or all calls.

Selecting on the call hyperlink will take you to the summary details for that individual call.

2.1.4 Issues Tab

Customer Service Workbench Issues lists the Customer Service issues.  There is a link to the issue details, and links to the details of the call or plot to which the issue relates.

Select the Issues Tab. 

There are also several filter options on the issues screen. In the simple filters you can choose to show just open, just closed, just new or all issues.

Selecting on the issue hyperlink will take you to the summary details for that issue:

 

2.1.5 Tasks Tab

Customer Service Workbench Tasks lists the Customer Service tasks.  There is a link to the task details, and links to the details of the call or plot to which the task relates.

Select the Tasks Tab. 

There are also several filter options on the tasks screen. In the simple filters you can choose to show just open, just closed, just overdue or all tasks. You can also filter by task ref., development number, date, completion date, call no. and task reason.

Selecting on the task hyperlink will take you to the summary details for that task:

 

2.1.6 Unscheduled Tab

Unscheduled Tasks shows any tasks that have been assigned to the TBA (Dummy) Operative, or that have no date and time allocated. The Task Scheduling button opens the scheduling screen in a separate window. 

Select the Unscheduled Tab. 

There are also several filter options on the tasks screen.
