Background

HM Revenue and Customs has a policy of moving taxpayers away from paper filing of tax returns to HMRC, and towards electronic filing. Starting with returns for the 2009/2010 tax year, all employers (subject to some minor exceptions which would not apply to clients) are compelled to complete their Payroll year-end submissions electronically.  Previously this only applied to employers with more than 50 employees in a single tax scheme.

For some time now, it has been possible to file PAYE in-year movements for payroll s and s (P45 and P46) electronically. From 6th April 2009, this has been compulsory for employers with over 50 employees; it is currently expected that this will become compulsory for smaller employers from 6th April 2011.

It should be noted that all forms P45 and P46 for payroll s and s must now include the employee's date of birth and gender. This applies to the paper forms as well as to electronic submissions.

General information about PAYE online filing obligations and facilities is available from the HMRC website at http://www.hmrc.gov.uk/paye/onlinefiling-understanding.htm.
