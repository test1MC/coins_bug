Exchange Variance

 

This is a standard accounting concept. If you receive an invoice today from a who you work with in Russian Roubles (RUB), your account and invoice document will be in RUB. Assume the exchange rate today with GBP is 50. An invoice for RUB 50,000 will be held as this value in account and document currencies, but in base will be converted to GBP 1,000.

When you subsequently come to pay the invoice some time later, the exchange rates may have changed. The rate may now be 40. You still need to pay the Russian RUB 50,000, but this is now GBP 1,250, so there is a difference of GBP 250. The cost of this is accounted for in the Exchange Variance account in the General Ledger postings. There are a range of points in where this account can be posted to.

Unrealed Exchange Variance

Because the exchange rate movements such as above can be significant, it is normal to check periodically the transactions held on the system in foreign currencies, to get early warning of significant differences, and to make provisions for them.

You can report on the unrealed variance using the Unrealed Variance Reports in the appropriate ledger.

Using the Unrealed Variance Reports, you can also create unrealed variance journals. These are balance sheet liability transactions, which are backed out in the following period. They can give you early warning of significant exchanges losses (or gains!). Unrealed variance amounts produced by these journals are posted to the unrealed variance account specified on the currency record.
