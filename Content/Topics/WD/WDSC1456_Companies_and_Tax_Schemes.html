A scheme in CIS terms is a reporting entity as registered with HMRC.  If , divisions or branches share the same UTR, tax office reference and Accounts Office Reference, they belong to the same scheme.  Separate schemes frequently share the same UTR but have different Tax Office References and Accounts Office References.

The most common configuration is to have each as a separate tax scheme.  can handle more than one scheme in a , or a scheme that uses a number of , or you can mix the two approaches, as it is how the payments resolve into schemes that matters. 

You will only be able to make one submission per scheme in any one period.   This means that you must not have the same scheme in more than one unless the have been linked for CIS tax purposes (see More Than One Scheme in a ).
