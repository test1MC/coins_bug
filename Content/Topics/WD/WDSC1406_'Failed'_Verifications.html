"Failed" Verifications

If the verification process (using "Match" mode) returns the status of a subcontractor as "Failed", this means the data you hold in is not sufficiently similar to the data that HMRC holds.  This may be because:

  • The subcontractor is not actually registered with HMRC. 
    You may want to check with the subcontractor whether they are registered, and if not, whether they want you to verify them as unmatched and pay them immediately at the higher rate of deduction, or whether they want to register first so that you can pay them gross or at the lower rate of deduction.

    If the subcontractor is not registered and you want to verify them as unmatched, re-submit the record for verification (using "Verify" mode) in Verification.  This should return a verification status of unmatched, which will allow you to pay the subcontractor.

    If the subcontractor is going to register with HMRC, wait until the subcontractor notifies you that they are registered, then re-submit the record for verification (using either "Match" or "Verify" mode) in Verification.  This should return a verification status of matched and the tax treatment, which will allow you to pay the subcontractor.

  • There are discrepancies between the data in and the HMRC data.

    In this case, update the data in Subcontractor Maintenance, then re-submit the record for verification (using either Match" or "Verify" mode; we recommend "Match") in Verification.  This should return a verification status of "Matched" and the tax treatment, which will allow you to pay the subcontractor.
