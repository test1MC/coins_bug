Tax Point Dates

The tax point dates that uses for transactions in the Return are as follows:

The tax point is the invoice date. updates the either when the invoice is registered, or when the invoice is costed, depending on the VATCOSTparameter.

The tax point is the invoice date. updates when the invoice is posted.

Subcontractors Ledger

  • If the billing type is Invoice, the tax point is the Certificate date. is updated when the certificate is posted.
  • If the billing type is Authenticated Receipt or Self-Billing, the tax point is the number of days after the date determined by the CHQ+DAYS or SB+DAYS parameter. is updated when the payment for the certificate is posted.

Ledger

If the is handled on an Invoice or Receipt basis, the tax point is the certificate date. is updated when the certificate is entered. Note: the Receipt option is for use when you only receive cash from a customer and wish to enter a certificate as a receipt.

If the is handled on a Certificate basis, the tax point is the Deposit Date. is updated when the payment for the certificate is posted. You should use this option if the is handled on an Authenticated Receipt basis.

The tax point for payments is the date, and for receipts it is the batch transaction date. is updated when the batch is posted.

General Ledger

The tax point is the transaction date. is updated when the batch is posted.

Expenses

The tax point is the expense date. is updated when the expense claim batch is posted.

House Sales

For House Sales journals, the tax point date is the journal date.  For completion statements, this should be the  legal completion date.   is updated when the journal is posted.

 

When creating Returns, includes all transactions with a tax point date on or before the date of the return, provided the has been updated as noted above.  Transactions that would have been included but where the update has not happened (for example, an invoice dated in March has been input but is not posted until after the Return has been made) will be included in the following Return.
