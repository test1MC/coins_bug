Offer Calculation

You can use Offer Calculation to record the basis of the offer you are going to make on a part-exchange property.  can help you to calculate the offer by taking into account various values associated with the part-exchange, such as the four-week selling price, an average of the valuations, the estate agent's fees, the valuation fees, and so on.

You can then produce an offer letter to send to the customer.

To calculate an offer:

  1. Go to the part-exchange summary.
  2. Click the More > tab, then the Offer Calculation tab.

    3. If there is an existing current offer, you must first cancel the offer (see below) before adding a new one.

  3. Use the Add Type drop-down to choose the type of calculation and click .

    4. (There may be different types of calculation for different types of property; for example you might use one calculation for a part-exchange against a plot and another for a part-exchange against a part-exchange.)

  4. If necessary enter the deadline date and any notes about the offer.

    NOTE

    The values will not show in the offer calculation until you have saved the calculation record.
  5. Click .

    6. The Lines tab shows the detail of the calculation. 

  6. If part of the calculation is based on values you supply, click next to the line, enter the value and click .
  7. When you have entered any values you need to, and the calculation is complete, click .

    8. will warn you if you do not have the required number of valuations recorded before you create an offer.
