Purchase Ordering Configuration

Purchase Ordering in its fullest form consists of order entry (materials, and subcontract), GRN (delivery note) processing and allocation, tracking subcontract agreements, and matching of invoices to GRNs.

For smaller sites and short projects, the overheads in terms of time, cost and organation may not justify the processing of GRNs or detailed sheet allocations. If this is the case, set the Accrual method field on the record (in the Status Ledger) to O. GRNs will then not be required and accruals will be based on the due dates of orders.

There are two ways to use Purchase Ordering:

  • Linked directly to the ledgers (and Subcontractors Ledger).
  • Linked to the ledgers through the Company Information Workbench (this is the recommended approach to get full benefit from the Purchase Ordering functionality).

To use the Company Information Workbench, set the parameter USECIS in each of the ledgers to Y. Use the function parameters for individual procedures to determine whether the procedure links to the Company Information Workbench or the ledgers.
