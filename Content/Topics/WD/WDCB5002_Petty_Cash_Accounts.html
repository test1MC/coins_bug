Petty Cash Bank Accounts

Petty cash bank accounts are accounts which can update either a single or a set of General Ledger codes, and which can only be accessed by specified users. Petty cash bank account security prevents petty cash users from having access to all other banks.

Petty Cash bank accounts linked to a will more than likely be used by site-based cashiers while those linked to General Ledger codes will likely relate to office petty cash.

  • Petty cash users will only be able to enter payment and receipt transactions for the bank account(s) they are set up against.
  • The users will only be able to enter payment and receipt transactions to the or GL codes specified in the petty cash bank maintenance record.
  • The users will only see batches or transactions for the bank account(s) they are set up against.

To set up a petty cash account:

  1. Go to Bank Accounts.
  2. Open the record for the bank account you want to set up as a petty cash account.
  3. the Petty Cash Bank Account box.
  4. Enter a list of the users who will have access to the account.
  5. Choose the Security Type (whether the account updates a or the General Ledger).
  6. Enter the or a General Ledger account mask, as appropriate.
  7. Save the record.

To change or remove the security settings, use the Uncheck Petty Cash Bank action.
