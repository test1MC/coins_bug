Ground Rent and Service Charges

House Sales allows you to bill ground rent, service charges and other charges automatically.  Normally the bill is produced based on information held against the plot, though you can also enter charges manually.  This is intended for simple ground rent during the period before the freehold is sold ? it is not designed as a full rental ledger.

  • Standard ground rent and/or service charge calendars can be set up for each to handle monthly, quarterly, half-yearly or annual due dates.
  • Rents and/or service charges may be charged either in advance or in arrears.
  • The annual ground rent and/or service charge amount is input and held against each plot.
  • For charges in advance, can automatically calculate the amount due on legal completion and include this in the completion statement.
  • A batch process is run periodically to generate ground rent and/or service charge charges for each plot due.
  • Manual charges or adjustments can be input.
  • Invoices are produced via a mail-merge routine to Microsoft Word.  Statements and reminder letters can also be produced via mail-merge to Microsoft Word.
  • Cash is input via the and allocated to charges.
  • Standard reports are available for balances, charges and unallocated cash.  Information is available for through the Financials options within Sales Workbench, and is also available in configurable reports.

To process rent and charges:

  1. Go to Charge Input / Process.
  2. Enter the batch header details.
  3. To add ground rent or service charge transactions:
    1. From the Options menu, select Process Ground Rent / Service Charges.
    2. Select which charges to process, and which dates to process for.

      The Period determines the to which the entries will be posted.  The Date range will determine the Rents and/or Service Charges processed: charges in arrears will be included if the end date is in the range; charges in advance will be included if the start date is in the range.  Charges already processed will not be processed again even if the same selection criteria are used, so it is safe to do a further run to pick up any charges that were not set-up at the time of an earlier run.

  4. To add other charges, add the transctions manually.
  5. Post the charge batch as normal.  This will generate the House Sales journal from which the General Ledger and/or Status postings are generated.

To print the invoices:

Either use Print House Sales Journals with suitable selection criteria or use New Charge Mail Shot with suitable selection criteria.  Note that, by either method, one invoice will be produced for each charge.  Therefore, if both rent and service charge are due, two invoices will be produced.

Rent and charges - configuration

On each plot, set up the amount of the ground rent or service charge:

  1. Go to Maintenance.
  2. Click the record link.
  3. Choose the Plot tab.
  4. Open the plot record.
  5. On the plot details screen, enter the annual Ground Rent and/or Service Charge.
  6. Save the plot record.

If billing periods are different across , set up the billing frequency on the :

  1. Go to Maintenance.
  2. Click the record link.
  3. Choose the More> tab, then the Ground Rent Periods or Service Charge Periods tab.
  4. Enter records on the Periods tab to say when each payment is due.  will divide the annual payment across the number of payment periods.

On any where Billing Periods have not been set up, the standard Billing Periods will be used.
