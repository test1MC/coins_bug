Joint Payments

In some countries, such as the US, subcontractors or can, under some circumstances, force a construction company to include them on the payments that the construction company receives from its customers. The term used to describe this is Joint Payees: the customer will make out a  to two companies: the construction company and its subcontractor or . 

Joint payee functionality in allows you to record the joint payees on invoices, and issue for joint payees.

If the joint payees functionality is enabled, when you enter the invoice, you record the amounts invoiced for the joint payees.  When you process payments for the invoice, will issue separate payments as appropriate: to the , and to the AND the joint payee.

Joint Payments

  1. When entering an invoice that requires joint payees:
    1. Set the Payment Type to Separate.Add Invoice Screen

    2. On the Joint Payees tab, enter the amounts each of the joint payees should receive. Add Invoice – Joint Payees Tab

      NOTE

      For that are linked to subcontractors, the Joint Payees tab is available only if subcontractor is exempt from tax (tax scheme = 'X').
  2. When selecting an invoice for payment, the payment amounts will default from the invoice record (if adding a new payment), but you can update the payment amounts for each payee. (Update will only be allowed if the tax deducted value is zero. This is because part payment of transactions with tax is not allowed.)

    If you use the Pay Invoice action for a part-paid certificate, will only copy the invoice amount and you will have to update the joint payment amounts manually.

  3. When freezing the payment batch, creates separate payment records; one for the and one with each of the joint payees. For example:

    For an invoice for 1000.00 with 20.00 discount from Browns Ltd

    Joint Payees: Jones and Sons 100.00 / A J Smith 200.00

    would produce three payments:
    680.00 with a payee name "Browns Ltd"
    100.00 with a payee name "Browns Ltd AND Jones and Sons"
    200.00 with a payee name "Browns Ltd AND A J Smith"

Configuration

Configuring

You can use the following fields on PL and SC to print the or subcontractor payee name, with the joint payee name if appropriate:

Field Display
RO_scs_jointPayeen Subcontractor payee name and joint payee name when relevant.
RO_scs_jointPayeen* As above with trailing ***.
RO_acs_jointPayeen  payee name and joint payee name when relevant.
RO_acs_jointPayeen* As above with trailing ***.

For payments that are factored, you can use the fields RO_actual_avm_jointPayeen, RO_actual_avm_jointPayeen*, RO_actual_sbm_jointPayeen, or RO_actual_sbm_jointPayeen* to print the actual subcontractor or name.
