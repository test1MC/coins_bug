Overview of SRM

Relationship Management provides a structured view of the and subcontractors that make up your company's supply chain. Other principal participants in the procurement process, such as your projects and the trades and commodities that you deal with, are also given particular focus.

Relationship Management sits "above" data already present in the Central Repository (which is a prerequisite for Relationship Management ).

Special emphasis is placed on the various approvals levels that you have allocated to your and subcontractors. So, for example, if you have categored your into "Supply Chain Partners", "Preferred " and "Approved " this can be specifically reflected in the SRM Workbench. The Search capability allows you to find and subcontractors efficiently. The Group Spend features provides you with a tool to trading patterns with your supply chain.

The Projects feature provides access to all aspects of the and in your system. The Performance section will be of special interest, for example, when evaluating how well your supply chain is performing on a particular .

The SRM Workbench also provides a "Category Management" view of your supply chain broken down by Trades, Materials and .  This allows you to focus on particular parts of your business.  So, for example, you can readily see how many preferred subcontractors are on file that carry out brickwork. This workbench also provides you with a Spend analysis broken down by trade, material and codes.

The ability to maintain Trading Agreements means that you can directly influence how purchase orders are raised as well as monitor non-compliance.

Performance related data can be accessed in many ways depending upon your starting point. So, for example, you may choose to dwell on how well your subcontractors are performing in one particular trade.  On another occasion, you might want to see how subcontractors on the whole are performing and then dig down to greater detail.
