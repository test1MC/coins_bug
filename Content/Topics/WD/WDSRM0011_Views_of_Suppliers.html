One of the key decisions when implementing SRM is the level at which you want to set the status code. 

If your business manages your supply chain centrally, you can assign status codes to a or subcontractor at a group level. This "Group Status Code" will be displayed in the Company Workbenches and lookups, and will be visible by users in each you have set up.

You will then also be able to assign a status code to the or subcontractor that is specific to your . This is the " Status Code".

Alternatively, you could choose not to set the status at a particular level.  This would allow you to use separate group and company Status Codes.

Setting your view level

Because all the screens in SRM need to show a consistent view, rather than setting your user view using a selector on the browse, you need to use Select Level to change your view level; any SRM Workbench screen that relies on view levels then uses this view level until you change it. This only affects your own view in SRM Workbench: it does not affect other users' views, and does not affect your user view outside SRM.
