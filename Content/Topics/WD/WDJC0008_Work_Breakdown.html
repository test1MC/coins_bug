Work Breakdown

In , work breakdown analysis provides a way of referring to specific activities or operations within a . For example, when raising requisitions, purchase orders or goods received notes, work breakdown structure (WBS) codes identify where materials are used and what for.

You can import project planning information from other software.

The components of the work breakdown analysis are:

  • A section, which typically represents a physical location within the scope of the .
  • An activity, which represents a type of work.
  • A WBS code, which represents an individual operation on a .

When configuring a , you can decide whether or not to use WBS codes. This will depend on the amount of information about activities you want to keep in Status. If you are using WBS codes, you must use either sections or activities, or both.

Work breakdown codes

WBS codes can be quite detailed and some reports (for example: Delivery Analysis Report (Material), Allocation Analysis, Sheet Report) allow you to sort by WBS. However, if you do not need to at this level of detail, you can treat WBS codes as being equivalent to combinations of sections and activities, which is what many users do.
