Due Dates, Discount Dates and Dates

Several procedures calculate one date based on another (for example, the due date for payment based on the date of the invoice, or dates for reports based on the current date). The way these dates are calculated depends on the Type and Days settings that have been set up.

For example, in , the due dates are calculated according to the Due Type and Due Days on the record.

The Type field controls how the Days field is interpreted. If the type is N, the Days represent a fixed number of days from the date of the invoice. If the Type is a number, it represents the number of months from the date of the invoice, and the Days represent a date in the month.

  • If the due date is a fixed number of days from the date of the invoice, enter N in the Due Type field, and the number of days in the Due Days field. For example, N 30 on invoice date 25/08/1808/25/18 would give a due date of 24/09/1809/24/18.
  • If the due date is a fixed date in the month, enter a number in the Due Type field to represent the number of months from the invoice date and enter the date in that month in the Due Date field. For example, 2 15 on invoice date 25/08/1808/25/18 would give a due date of 15/10/1810/15/18. A type of 0 means the month of the invoice.
  • If the due date is the last day of a month, enter the number of months from the invoice date in the Due Type field, and 31 in the Due Date field.
Type Days  
N 30 Due date is 30 days after the invoice date.
1 15 Due date is 15th of the month after the invoice date.
2 31 Date is the last day of the month, 2 months after the invoice date.
