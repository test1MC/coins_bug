Data Migration - Assumptions and Requirements

have assumed that in order to carry out the data transfer that the following actions will have been carried out by the client.

  • Configuration and parameter settings have been agreed and signed off.
  • Other setup tables are in place, such as Transaction Types, Budget Types, Memo Data Types, etc.
  • Coding structures such as:
    1. General Ledger codes
    2. Contract Status and any Sections and Activities
    3. Procurement Commodities, Trades, Labour Codes and Plant items
    4. Payroll Pay Items

have been agreed, signed off and in place.

  • Forms for printing P/L Remittances, S/C certificates/Remittances, Sales Ledger Invoices/Statements, Purchase Orders, etc. have been configured.
  • The initial checking and subsequent full reconciliation of the data to be transferred.
  • The signing off of the data transfer as fully reconciled by the client and that they accept full responsibility for the reconciliation, prior to any live transactions being entered onto the environment.

Any transactional data load will require a process to ensure that all loaded data reconciles. There must be controls in place that demonstrate that the data loaded agrees with the data from the legacy system.
