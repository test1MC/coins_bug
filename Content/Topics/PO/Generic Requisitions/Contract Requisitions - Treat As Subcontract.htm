Requisitions - Treat As Subcontract

It may be that a requisition cannot be ordered on a material or order nor fulfilled from , but the request should result in the raising of a subcontract order.

A new option - Treat As Subcontract - is available if the requisition has been issued but no orders have been raised against it.

The option marks the requisition as “Closed (Treated as Subcontract)”; no further processing occurs and no orders can be placed against it. A subcontract order would have to be raised manually.

Each line belonging to the requisition is also marked as “Treated as Subcontract”.

An additional option - Undo Treat as Subcontract - is available to undo the “Treated as Subcontract” status.

These two options do not appear on the Actions menu on the Requisitions Browse screen. This is because the decision to “Treat as Subcontract” would require that the buyer looks in detail into the requirement specified on the requisitions lines.

These options are available for Requisitions and also for all major types of requisition except subcontract (in other words, for material, , overhead, workshop, asset and ).
