Customer Statements

Module: Accounts Receivable

This document will demonstrate how to print customer statements for accounts receivable, service, and job receivable invoices. Statements can be printed for one or more customers as well as for future amounts. (the total of outstanding invoices whose due date is later than the aging date).

 

  1. To print a customer statement, go to Accounts Receivable> Customers> Customer Maintenance and hyperlink in on the desired customer.

Figure 1: Customer Maintenance


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Figure 2: Print Statement Box


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Under the Statement Tab, make sure the Print Statement Box is checked. If it is not, click open in bottom left of the screen, check the box, and click save in the bottom right of the screen. If the box is not check statements for this customer will not print.

  • Statement Cycle- Custom cycles can be created by entering characters into this field. When the statement gets printed the cycle can be entered and all customers with the cycle will get printed. **Note** There is no lookup for cycles. They will have to be kept track of off line.
  • Statement Address- Enter the statement address. If no address is entered, the address stored in the central repository will default in.

Click Save.

  1. To include zero balance invoices, go to Accounts Receivable> Cash Receipts> Enter Statement.

The far right shows invoices that are selected to print on the statement. To select an invoice with a zero balance, open the line and save it. To unselect an invoice, open the line, uncheck the box, and then save the line.

  1. Go to Accounts Receivable >Cash Receipts >Print Statements

Figure 6: Print Statements


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  1. Under the Main tab, enter in the appropriate data.

 

  • Form- Select the desired form for the statement format.
  • Statement Date- The date that will appear on the statement. Defaults to today.
  • Aging Date- The date against which aged debts are to be calculated.
  • Statement Cycle- To print statements for customers belonging to one statement cycle only, enter the statement cycle code. To print statements for all selected customers, leave blank
  • Statement by-  Whether to print a statement by customer, job, or division.
  • Date Sequence- Whether the statement will be ordered per invoice date or due date.
  • Include Customers in Credit- Whether to include customers whose balance is less than 0 on the statement.
  • Include Zero Balance Invoices- Whether to include zero balance invoices.
  • Include Job Related Invoices- Whether to include job related invoices.
  • Cleared Payments After- If showing cleared checks on the statement, only checks dated after this date will be included.
  • Exclude Statements with Balances Less Than- Stop statements with a minimal balance from printing by entering a value here.
  1. Select the Customer Selection Tab and enter in a customer. Then click Next.
