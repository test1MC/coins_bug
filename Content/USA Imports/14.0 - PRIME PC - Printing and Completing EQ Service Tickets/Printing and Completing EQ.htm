Printing and Completing EQ Service Tickets

Module: Equipment Manager (PC)

This document walks through the process of completing, closing, and posting EQ Service Tickets, and in the case of a recharge, printing the subsequent AR Invoice.

  1. Now that all expected costs have hit this Ticket, and if applicable, all changes to the recharge lines have been made, the Service Ticket can be completed.
  1. On the EQ Service Ticket Detail Tab, click the Open button.
  1. Click the “Completed” Checkbox, enter the Completed “On” date, and include the name of the Technician that completed the work.
  1. If applicable, click the “Ready to Recharge” checkbox, and add in any additional information in the Description fields. These fields can print on the invoice.
  1. When finished, click Save in the lower right corner.

Figure 1: Opened EQ Service Ticket Detail Tab with Completion Details

 

  1. Back on the EQ Service Ticket workbench, select the right Service Ticket’s row. Using the Action Bar at the bottom of the page, Post the EQ Service Ticket.

Figure 2: Using Action Bar to Post EQ Service Ticket

 

  1. Click Post will bring up the Job Order Cards Posting screen. Fill out the appropriate details here. Many of the fields are here to allow for posting multiple Service Tickets at once, and may not be applicable for a one-ticket posting process.
  • Choose the correct Fiscal Period
  • Type – from the Tickets selected to post, whether to actually post only Internal, External, or all tickets.
  • No Close - Whether to include incomplete EQ service tickets into the posting. Leaving this unchecked will not include incomplete tickets.
  • Tax Point – Only used in the UK for Tax purposes. In the US, this will simply change the transaction/invoice date.
  • Transaction Date – defines the transaction date for any incomplete ticket in the posting selection. This defaults in as today’s date, but can be changed.
  • When all details are sorted, click Next in the lower right corner.

 

Figure 3: Posting Details

 

  1. Back on the EQ Service Ticket Workbench, the Ticket now shows as having been charged, and, with the use of a column set option to include status, this Ticket’s status now shows as “6-Closed”.

Figure 4: EQ Service Ticket Workbench Showing updated Ticket

 

  1. If Recharge was selected, this posting process has automatically produced an AR Invoice Batch containing an invoice to the Customer specified in the Recharge analysis on the Service Ticket.
  1. To see this invoice, first go to Accounts Receivable > Invoices > Invoice & Credit Note Entry, and look for a recent batch called Workshop Recharge, with a batch type of RCHG.

Figure 5: AR Invoice Workbench with Recharge Batch Highlighted

 

  1. Hyperlinking into the invoice within this recharge batch shows the Invoice Details

Figure 6: AR Invoice Details

 

  1. Depending on the form details, the printed invoice may look like this. Note that the additional details entered in Step #4 were included in the creation of the AR Invoice, and in fact have printed out at the bottom.

Figure 7: Printed AR Invoice
