Processing EQ Service Tickets

Module: Equipment Manager (PC)

The EQ Service Ticket Workbench allows the user to create a service ticket for an asset, and manage all of the charges for the completion of the work. This can optionally be recharged to the Job or the Customer in full or part charge.

 

  1. To add a new EQ Service Ticket, go to Equipment Manager > Workshop > EQ Service Tickets, and click Add.

Figure 1: Clicking Add on the EQ Service Ticket Workbench

 

  1. Enter in the details on the Service Ticket Detail page. The mandatory fields are marked below with an underline.
  • Workshop - the inventory location/depot/workshop where the asset is kept
  • Asset - the code for the asset on which the work will be done
  • Event – select the type of Workshop activity to be done; typically Repair or Service
  • Events are set up using Fixed Assets > Setup > Maintenance > Workshop Events.
  • Title – a brief description of this Service Ticket
  • This will auto populate from the Event, but can be changed
  • Reg No/Meter Reading - the registration number and meter reading of the Asset
  • Date - the date that the service will be done
  • This defaults in as today’s date.
  • Order - the customer’s order number, if relevant
  • Received - the date that the workshop received the order
  • Required - the date by which the work is required to be completed
  • Authorized - the person who authorized the workshop EQ service ticket
  • Estimate/Reference - the estimation value of workshop, and its reference
  • Location Type - the location type where the work is to be undertaken (Workshop, Site or External).
  • Description - the detailed description of the work to be done
  • Recharge - whether costs to this workshop EQ service ticket get recharged
  • Charged - whether this workshop EQ service ticket has been fully charged
  • Ready To Charge - whether the workshop EQ service ticket is ready to charge
  • Recharge To - the type of analysis (customer, job, or split)
  • The following field will be the number of the customer or the job.
  • Charge Center - the charge center
  • Description - the recharging description
  • Status – this will be blank initially, and automatically move to Assigned when the resource is chosen, then Scheduled once the time slot is selected, then Work in Progress once work starts, and so on
  • Hold - a user defined hold code to record why work is being held up
  • Completed - whether the workshop EQ service ticket has been completed – Leave blank until work is completed
  • - on - the date that the work was completed
  • - by - the person who completed the workshop EQ service ticket

 

Figure 2: Details on Add EQ Service Ticket Page

 

  1. When all details are correct, click Save in the lower right corner. [Figure 2]

 

This Service Ticket is now ready to accept costs from different sources, such as Workshop Purchase Orders, Inventory Issues, Payroll, or Journals. This document will focus on Issuing Inventory and Raising Workshop PO’s.

 

  1. To issue inventory to an EQ Service Ticket, Go to Inventory > Input Inventory Transactions, and click Add to add a batch.

Figure 3: Clicking Add on the Inventory Transaction Entry – Batch Page

  1. In the Batch tab, complete the batch header details:
  • Check that the fiscal period and transaction dates are correct
  • Transaction Type should be “I – Inventory Issue”
  • Enter a Batch Description
  • Select the Inventory Location from which the inventory is being issued

 

Figure 4: Inventory Batch Header Details

  1. When all details are correct, click Save in the lower right corner. [Figure 4]
  1. Now in the Transaction Tab, click Add.
  1. Fill out the Inventory Line details:
  • Inventory Code – Enter or select the Inventory Item number. The Description will auto-populate from the inventory code, but can be overwritten.
  • Enter the Quantity. The Unit Price will auto-populate as well, and the GL Amount will show the total price.
  • Select “Workshop” from the Analysis dropdown.
  • The analysis may be partially completed, if the default cost type for Workshop was defined on the Inventory Group. If this is the case, do not overwrite. Simply enter the EQ Service Ticket number before the cost type, or select it from the lookup. The full analysis string should look like EQServiceTicket#.WorkshopCostType.
  • Check the transaction date, and enter a reference number if needed.
  • Adding a responsible person is optional, but the line must have a valid tax code before hitting the Save button on the left.

Figure 5: Clicking Save on the Fully Detailed Inventory Transaction Line

 

 

 

  1. Continue to add lines on this screen as needed, and click the Next button in the lower right corner when finished.
  1. Post the Inventory Issue batch by using the action bar on the bottom of the Inventory Transaction Entry workbench.

Figure 6: Posting Inventory Issue Batch with Action Bar

 

 

  1. To see these costs on the Service Ticket, view the EQ Service Ticket Costs Tab

 

Figure 7: EQ Service Ticket Costs Tab on the Service Ticket Record

If the intention is to order parts for the service directly, without putting the parts into inventory, this can be done by raising a Workshop order directly against the EQ Service Ticket.

 

  1. To add a Workshop Order, go to Procurement > More Orders > Workshop Orders, and click the Add button.

Figure 8: Clicking Add on the Workshop Orders Workbench

 

  1. On the Main Tab, fill out the details of the Order Header.
  • Workshop Ref – Choose the appropriate EQ Service Ticket
  • Supplier/Office – Select the correct Supplier and Office
  • Ensure the Order Date is correct, and fill in the appropriate Due Date
  • Add a description for what is being ordered.
  • Check the Requisition and Addresses Tabs, and click Save.

Figure 9: Workshop Order Header Details

 

  1. In the Order Lines screen that now appears, click Add to add a material line.
  1. Fill out the line details
  • Code – The commodity code of the material being ordered
  • The Description field can be overwritten if needed
  • Enter in Quantity, Price, and any Freight amount.
  • Click Save on the left side of the Material line.

Figure 10: Clicking Save on the Filled-Out Material Line

 

 

  1. Continue to add lines on this screen as needed, and click the Next button in the lower right corner when finished.
  1. Use the Action Bar at the bottom of the Workshop Orders workbench to commit this order.

Figure 11: Committing Workshop Order with Action Bar

  1. Looking at the EQ Service Ticket Costs again will not show this order’s cost. The value of the order is not real cost until the invoice is processed.
  1.   The Orders for EQ Service Ticket Tab will show all the details of all orders raised against this ticket.

Figure 12: Order Details on the Orders for EQ Service Ticket Tab

  1. When the order arrives, to properly receive the material, go to Procurement > More GRNs > Workshop GRNs to enter the Goods Received Note (GRN).
  1. Click Add.

Figure 13: Clicking Add on the Workshop GRN Workbench

  1. Fill out the Header record details
  • Ticket No - The supplier's delivery note reference
  • Check the delivery date.
  • PO Number – Enter the Workshop Order number. The Sales Tax, Supplier, and Office info will pull in from the Purchase Order.
  • Add any applicable notes, if needed, and click Save

Figure 14: Clicking Save on Filled-out Workshop GRN Header

  1. After clicking Save, the GRN Lines screen will appear. All of the material lines from the Workshop Order are displayed, with the quantity expected from the order.
  1. Highlight the line or lines, and using the Action Bar at the bottom of the screen, Accept the Whole Quantity ordered. Once this action processes, the Quantity Delivered column will update to show the now delivered amount.

Figure 15: Using Action Bar in GRN Lines Screen to Accept Order

  1. Once the AP Invoice, fully matched to this Workshop Order, is processed and posted, the costs will hit the EQ Service Ticket Costs Tab.

Figure 16: EQ Service Ticket Costs Tab on the Service Ticket Record

 

  1. When all costs have hit this Service Ticket, there are two options:
  • If applicable, move on to Recharging the costs.
  • If not, complete and print the Ticket.
