Adding an Asset

Module: Equipment Manager (PC)

This document demonstrates how to add an asset in COINS.

 

  1. Go to Equipment Manager > Assets and click Add.

Figure 1: Adding an Asset


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  1. Fill in the appropriate fields.

Figure 2: Details Tab


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  1. Under the Details tab enter:
  • Asset - the code by which the asset will be identified by(can be letter or numbers)
  • Group - the asset group
  • Description - a description of the asset
  • Make, Model, and Year - optional
  • Department - the asset department. The asset departments are used to identify financial information regarding assets and costing. This will typically set the first portion of the GL.
  • Home Depot- where the asset is based
  • Current Depot- the current location of the asset
  • Ownership Status – informational field
  • Ownership Charged to Date – enter date if utilizing supplemental ownership charges
  1. Ignore the Cargo Attributes and Transporter Attributes sections.
  1. Fill out any other fields you have information for such as supplier

**Note: The Part of field is if the asset you are adding is part of an existing asset. For example, if you are adding a plow on to a truck, or a crane on the back of a truck.

 

  1. The Purchase tab can be skipped. If you don’t enter info here, you can directly link the invoice reconciliation to the asset.
  1. Next, go to the Analysis Tab, where options for user-defined analysis sets may be specified.

Figure 3: Analysis Tab


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  1. Select between Y-Capitalized and N-Not Capitalized. Often the asset will be capitalized. This analysis set can be utilized to make running depreciation a quicker process by filtering out assets (such as small tools) that are not capitalized.

 

  1. Next, go to the Depreciation tab. Info here will default from the Asset Group specified.
  1. Select the appropriate Depreciation Method.
  1. Initial WDV, Current WDV, and Start Date should be left blank, as these will be filled in later during the invoice reconciliation process.

Figure 4: Depreciation Tab


 

  1. Then click Save in the bottom, right corner of the screen.
