Create Location Customer

Module: Service Management

After creating a service location the next step is to create a location customer. This ties together customers from Accounts Receivable and the locations in the service management module. Once these are tied together a customer and location can be tied to a service order. A Location is where the work is performed and the Customer is who pays the bill.

 

  1. In the Tree Navigation to the left of the screen navigate to Service Management. Click on Locations from there. (In order to create a location customer a company must have already been created and they must already be set up as a customer in Accounts Receivable.)

Figure 1: Service Location Screen

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  1. Click into the Location name’s hyperlink. Once you have enter the location go to the second tab which is “Customers”. Then click the add button on the bottom.

Figure 2: Service Location Customer Screen

 

  1. Click into the ellipse “…” and add the customer account you are trying to create a customer location for. Fill in the information in the four tabs. Each selection is explained in detail in the following screen shots.

Figure 3: Beginning of Entering Location Customer

  1. On the first tab “Main” it is mandatory to put in Customer Account. This will pull over the Main Address information from the customer that was already created. The other mandatory fields are “Invoice Consolidation” and “Invoice Bill To Address Source”.

Figure 4: Customer General Information

Location Phone is the number that will prepopulate when you choose this customer account for a service order.

Invoice Consolidation: If sending multiple invoices to a customer, would you like them sent Separate (S-Separate) or together (C-Customer)

Affiliation Type is how this customer account relates to the service location, examples may include LANDLORD or OWNER.

Primary customer is if this is the main customer account at this location, if there are multiple customers at this location the primary customer will automatically be pulled over when starting a service order at this location.

From and To is how long and for how long this customer will be associated with this location, can be left blank until ending a customer relationship at this location.

Figure 5: Customer Terms and Credit

On Hold is whether or not the customer is on hold for this location.

Due Type determines how long of a time period it is before money is due after the service happens.

Retention % determines whether a percentage will be held and billed at a later date.

Credit Limit and Credit Status are pulled over from the customer already being set up.

 

  1. The next tab is PO & Misc the required items on this section are “Require PO” in the purchase order section, “Revenue Taxable Tax Code” and “Revenue Exempt Tax Code” in the SE Revenue Tax Defaults, and finally the “Taxable Tax Code” in JS section Tax Defaults.

Figure 6: Customer Purchase Order Information

Requires PO – Do we need a PO options are N-Not required, S-Prior to saving service order, D-Prior to dispatching, B-Prior to billing.

Allowable PO Type – This is the type of service order that can be referenced for this customer.

Authorization Limit Default – This is the limit to default to a service order that does not refer to a blanket purchase order.

Figure 7: Customer Miscellaneous Information

Service Department – Select appropriate service department for the customer.

Service Order Group – Select appropriate service order group.

Response Type – Enter the code for the response type this can also be chosen at Service Order screen.

Invoice Delivery Method – should the invoice be Printed of Emailed.

Figure 8: Customer Quoted Rates

Quoted Rate - whether or not to include quoted rate categories for quoted rates.

Allowable categories – only optional when quoted rate box is checked.

Figure 9: SE Revenue Tax Defaults

SE Revenue Tax Defaults – if the customer will pay tax on any items on a Service Invoice.

Revenue Taxable Tax Code – select the appropriate tax code.

Revenue Exempt Tax Code – Select the appropriate non-taxable tax code.

Revenue Taxable – check off any of the following boxes if the associated item is taxable.

Figure 10: JS Section Tax Defaults

JS section Tax Defaults – whether or not tax will be paid on a material order or purchase order.

Taxable Tax Code – the tax code for the transaction.

Taxable – check this box if any of the associated items are taxable.

Figure 11: Customer Invoice Forms

Select the appropriate invoice tax forms.

Figure 12: Customer USA Tax Information

Enter the information associated with the payroll information for the tax localities.

Figure 13: Customer Canadian Tax Information

Enter the information associated with the payroll information for the tax localities.

Figure 14: Customer General Information

Typically, customers will fill out the State and Workers Comp State in this field. The rest can be blank unless instructed by Payroll to fill out.

 

  1. Click the SAVE button on the bottom right hand corner of the screen.
