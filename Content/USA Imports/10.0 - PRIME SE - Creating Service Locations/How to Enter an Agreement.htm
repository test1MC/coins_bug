How to Enter an Agreement with Financial Information

Module: Service Management

  1. First go to the tree menu. Go to Service Management > Agreements and hit the green “ADD” button on the bottom of the screen

Figure 1: Add Agreement

  1. Enter the agreement name at the top of the screen. On the left hand side enter the customer account. This can be whatever you would like, select the agreement type in the lookup for this example we will use PM. Agreement group, service manager and default service department are all optional fields.

Figure 2: First Beginning Entry Screen

  1. On the right hand side of the screen Inception date will equal the start date of the agreement. Default renewal duration is typically 12 months. Billing Type and revenue recognition method will usually be schedule, which means you send out the bill regardless or not if you have been there for service yet. The default billing and revenue schedules are lookup fields. This is the time frame for which the agreement should be scheduled to be billed or revenue generated, such as monthly or quarterly.

Figure 3: Second Beginning Entry Screen

  1. Under the analysis tab, complete the dropdown for Multi, which will typically be NO. Then save the agreement.

Figure 1: Caption Title

  1. Under the agreement, open the locations tab. From here you will add the location that will correspond with this agreement. When you enter the location, it will prefill the address, select the invoice type and bill to address type field, which is typically set to customer. The invoice delivery method will either be print of email. Save the line by using the floppy disk button to the left of the line.

Figure 5: Add Location

  1. Now hyperlink into the location name.

Figure 6: Begin to Add Type of Work

  1. Enter in the Type of Work. This will prefill most of the other information on the screen. If you are unsure of the type of work, you can use the “…” lookup field.

Figure 7: Add Type of Work

  1. The only other mandatory field to fill in will be the budget section. Create a number using ZZ followed by 6 digits of the agreement name (this can be either letters or numbers).

Figure 8: Add Budget Section

  1. You will get a pop-up warning message that the budget section is not on file and will be created. We expect and want this. Press okay.

Figure 9: Create Budget Section

  1. Now go to the Sales Rep Tab. You will need to add a Sales Rep for this agreement.

Figure 10: Create Sales Rep

  1. Now we need to create the cost codes. To create cost codes go to Operational Setup > Create Cost Codes and hit next. There will be a report created in the report viewer with two cost codes created. They should be your budget section followed by ACR and your budget section followed by APM.

Figure 11: Create Cost Codes

  1. Under the agreement, open up Operational Setup > Agreement Amount. This is where you will set up the totals for the agreement.

Figure 12: Agreement Amount

  1. Highlight the agreement amount on the drop down action bar click the arrow for both Create Revenue Schedule and Create Billing Schedule.

Figure 13: Creating Billing/Revenue Schedule

  1. Under Operational Setup > Billing Schedule check to verify the schedule was created

Figure 14: Billing Schedule

  1. Under Operational Setup > Revenue Schedule check to verify the schedule was created

Figure 15: Revenue Schedule

  1. Under Cost Budget, put in the costs for the entire year for each Cost Type Group in the first month of the agreement. The first line, “PM Labor” (HRS) is for hours for the entire year. The second line “PM Labor” (L) is for the money for labor for the entire year. To open a Cost Type to enter the planned hours or budget, click the folder open button to the left of it. Enter the information then hit the save button that is to the left of the line. The only time to enter something in the other months is if there was a contract revision.

Figure 16: Cost Budget Entry

  1. Now go to Operational Setup > Freeze Initial Budget. Open the line and freeze the budget so no changes can be made.

Figure 17: Freeze Initial Budget

  1. Go to Operational Setup > Budget Summary which will give you a review of the budget and costs.

Figure 18: Budget Summary

  1. Go to Operational Setup > Cost Types. This shows all the available cost types on the agreement.

Figure 19: Cost Types
