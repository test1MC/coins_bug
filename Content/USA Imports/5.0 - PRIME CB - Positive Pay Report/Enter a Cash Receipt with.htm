Enter a Cash Receipt with a Discount for a Invoice

Module:

This process allows users to enter a “discount taken by the customer” when cash is received against a invoice ( or modules).

For module invoices, an easier process can be used. See Discounts on Quick Cash Receipt Entry for Invoices.

The example in this scenario is meant to address:

  • There is a invoice for a certain amount (550.00 in this example).
  • The customer sends less than the amount of the invoice (500.00 in this example).
  • The user wants to take the entire invoice amount off of the report and out of the receivables balance.

Assumptions:

  • The will still get credit for the entire invoiced value (the user does not “back out” of the amount of the invoice that was discounted).
  1. Go to > Cash Transactions > Cash Transaction Entry (Figure 1).
  2. Click at the bottom of the screen to add a new batch (Figure 1).Figure 1: Click to Add a New Batch

  3. Set the appropriate and Effective Date (Figure 2).
  4. Select Manual for Manual/Print (Figure 2).
  5. Select Receipt for Payment Receipt (Figure 2).
  6. Select the box for Allocate? (Figure 2).
  7. Select 01-Operating for Bank Code (Figure 2).
  8. Then, click in the bottom, right corner of the screen (Figure 2).Figure 2: Enter Batch Information, Click Save

  9. Next, in the Transactions tab, click at the bottom of the screen (Figure 3).Figure 3: Click Add in Transactions tab

  10. Select Receipt as the Type (Figure 4).
  11. Select REC-Receipt as the T/Type (Figure 4).
  12. Select CS (for ) as the Ledger and select the appropriate Account () (Figure 4).
  13. Enter the Payer/Payee (Figure 4).
  14. For the Amount, enter the amount of money that was actually received, net of discount (i.e. less than the balance of the invoice) (Figure 4).
  15. Click to save (Figure 4).Figure 4: Enter Cash Receipt Information, Click Save

  16. At the next screen, in the Analysis Lines tab, the Amount entered in step 14 will be listed as the Amount and the Amount Left to Distribute (Figure 5).
  17. Click at the bottom of the screen (Figure 5).Figure 5: Click Add in Analysis Lines tab

  18. The first line needs to be added and saved with the following specifications (Figure 6):
    • Ledger: CS
    • Analysis: the number
    • Amount: balance of the invoice(s) paid
  19. Click to save the line (Figure 6).Figure 6: Enter and Save CS Analysis Line

  20. At the next screen, in the To Allocate tab, select the invoice to be paid with the discount, select Pay Certificate from the drop-down on the bottom action bar, and click (Figure 7). This will mark the invoice(s) as fully paid.
    • Or open each line using and enter the Amount Paid value manually.Figure 7: Pay Certificate, Apply Action

  21. The Payment Amount should equal Allocated, with nothing left as Unallocated (Figure 8).
  22. Click in the bottom, right corner of the screen (Figure 8).Figure 8: Payment Amount = Allocated, Click Next

  23. Back in the Analysis Lines tab, notice the Amount Left to Distribute (Figure 9).
  24. Click at the bottom of the screen to account for the discount (Figure 9).Figure 9: Amount Left to Distribute, Click Add in Analysis Lines tab

  25. Enter a second distribution line to cost the discount amount. Enter the discount taken by the customer as a negative number and select a GL Account or a /Cost Code to be debited with the expense, which should amount to the same as the Amount Left to Distribute (Figure 10):
    • To Debit the Expense to a GL Account (which will be done in this example):
      • Ledger: G
      • Analysis: the discounts expense account
      • Amount: *negative* value of discount taken by the customer (the Amount Left to Distribute)
    • To Debit the Expense to a /Cost Code:
      • Ledger: C
      • Analysis: ( Number).(Cost Code).(Cost Category) ->  (Example: 10098.903.O)
      • Amount: *negative* value of discount taken by the customer (the Amount Left to Distribute)
  26. Click to save the line (Figure 10).Figure 10: Enter Line to Debit Discount Expense, Click Save

  27. Now the Amount Left to Distribute should read 0.00 (Figure 11).
  28. Click in the bottom, right corner of the screen (Figure 11).Figure 11: Amount Left to Distribute = 0.00, Click Next

  29. At the next screen, in the Transactions tab, select the transaction, select Post in the drop-down on the bottom action bar, and click (Figure 12).Figure 12: Post the Transaction, Apply Action

  30. The GL Cost Posting Report for the transaction is seen in Figure 13:Figure 13: GL Cost Posting Report

  31. Next, go to > > Applications/Certificates (Figure 14).
  32. The invoice is now marked as Paid (Figure 14).Figure 14: Invoice is Marked as “Paid”
