Loading and Paying Credit Card Statement

Module: Accounts Payable

Once the credit card statement has been delivered it needs to be entered into the system. This document will show how to reconcile this statement with the invoices that should have already been posted for the credit cards. After the statement is reconciled the credit card statement will be paid.

  1. Load Credit Card Statement into COINS and reconcile. (Refer to the Bank Reconciliation document for in depth help with this process.) (Note if items on the credit card statement are not in the system either A) enter missing items action in bank reconciliation. B) Delete these items off the statement in coins through manually enter bank statement function. Then break the lines out when entering in the invoice for the statement.

Go to Cash Manager>Bank Reconciliation>Import Bank Statement

Figure 1: Import Credit Card Statement


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  • Bank- Select the credit card bank
  • Input File- Select the file with the correct credit card statement.

Click next.

  1. Go to Cash Manager> Bank Reconciliation>Bank Reconciliation

 

Figure 2: Bank Reconciliation


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Hyperlink on to the date.

Figure 3: Matching Unreconciled Items


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Select the matching transaction and click on the Reconcile Selected Items action button.

 

  1. Once the statement has been completely reconciled enter an invoice for the bank statement.

 

Figure 4: Creating invoice Batch


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Go to Accounts Payable>Invoices> Enter Invoices. Create an invoice batch for the credit card payment.

 

Figure 5: Invoice Details


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  • Supplier Account- The credit card company should be entered here. If the credit card company is not in the system, add it in the central repository, then pull it in at the suppliers workbench. (Refer to the Adding a Supplier how-to document) 
  • Supplier Reference- Enter the invoice number.
  • Analysis- Set the analysis to department and select the appropriate department that the credit card applies to.
  • Merchandise- Enter the amount on the credit card statement.
  • Sales/Use tax- Enter an exempt tax code.

Click Save.

  1. Next, on the Costing Distribution tab fill in the appropriate information.


Figure 6: Costing Distribution

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  • Enter the GL account associated with the credit card. If an error is generated (seen in the picture above) manual posting must be turned on. To do this go to General Ledger> GL Elements and find the credit card bank. Manual post is turned off. To allow manual post open the account, check the manual post box, and then save the line.

Figure 7: Allow Manual Post


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

To go back to the invoice costing distribution tab, go to Accounts Payable> Invoices> Enter Invoices. Click on the credit card batch. Then, click on the hyperlink of the invoice and finally click on the Costing Distribution tab.  Select the GL account again and no error will appear. Verify the Merchandise amount is the same as the total on the credit card statement.

Figure 8: Invoice Costing


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Save the line. Repeat for every line on your credit card statement.

  1. Post the invoice


Figure 9: Posting the Invoice

  1. If cutting a check to the credit card company, select this invoice for payment like any other invoice (refer to AP Manual Invoice Payment how- to document)
