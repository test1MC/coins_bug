Archiving and Unarchiving Purchase Orders

Module: Procurement

This document demonstrates the process of archiving and unarchiving purchase orders in COINS, and how it affects live workbenches and reports.

 

Archiving Purchase Orders

  1. Before archiving purchase orders in COINS, it is important to note that purchase orders will only be archived if they meet the following conditions when the Archive Orders function is performed:
  • The date of the original order is a certain amount of years ago, depending on the setting of the PODTLIVE parameter, which is explained in step #22.
  • If the order is a job order, the associated job is set to Complete.
  • The order itself is complete, and all associated GRNs and requisitions are complete.
  • All associated invoices are "costed" (that is, not on the register).

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  1. Go to Procurement > Administration > Archive Orders (Figure 1).
  1. Select the appropriate filter options for what orders to archive. For this example, a certain date range is selected for which orders to archive (Figure 1).
  1. Select the appropriate option for Mode (Figure 1):
  • Memorandum
  • If this option is selected, the report will simply show which orders would be archived, and it won’t archive the orders.
  • Confirm
  • If this option is selected, it will archive the orders.
  1. Click Next in the bottom, right corner of the screen (Figure 1).

Figure 1: Archive Orders

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Live Workbenches

  1. When an order is archived, it is removed from live workbenches in COINS. For example, an archived material order would not appear in Procurement > Material Orders.
  1. However, archived purchase orders can still be viewed in a different sub-folder. Go to Procurement > More Orders > Orders (Inc. Archived) (Figure 2).
  1. For each type of order (highlighted in Figure 2) in that sub-folder, the workbenches will show all orders (including archived orders).

Figure 2: Orders (Including Archived) Sub-Folder

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Reports

  1. Archived orders can be excluded or included when running reports.
  1. We will demonstrate using the Purchase Order Status (Jobs) Report. Go to Procurement > Reports > Orders > Purchase Order Status Reports > Jobs (Figure 3).
  1. For most reports in the Procurement module, there will be a field titled Show Orders. Select the appropriate option:
  • Live Only
  • If selected, the report will exclude archived orders.
  • All (Inc. Archived)
  • If selected, the report will include archived orders.
  1. For this report, go to the Include tab and select the appropriate option for Show Orders (Figure 3).
  • Note: The location/tab of the Show Orders field varies by report.
  1. Click Next in the bottom, right corner of the page to generate the report (Figure 3).

Figure 3: Include/Exclude Archived Orders from a Report

 

 

 

 

 

Unarchiving Purchase Orders

  1. Purchase orders can be reinstated from the archive at any time. To reinstate orders from archive, the process is very similar to archiving purchase orders. It is easy to unarchive and re-archive, if needed.
  1. Go to Procurement > Administration > Reinstate Orders from Archive (Figure 4).
  1. Select the appropriate filter options for what orders to reinstate. For this example, we are reinstating one specific order from archive (Figure 4).
  1. Select the appropriate option for Mode (Figure 4):
  • Memorandum
  • If this option is selected, the report will simply show which orders would be reinstated from archive, and it won’t reinstate the orders.
  • Confirm
  • If this option is selected, it will reinstate the orders from archive.
  1. Click Next in the bottom, right corner of the page (Figure 4).

Figure 4: Reinstate Orders from Archive

 

 

 

 

 

 

Relevant Parameters

  1. Go to Procurement > Setup > Configuration > PO Parameters (Figure 5).
  • In general, this is a good place for reference regarding how purchase orders work in COINS.
  1. Filter by ID and search for PODTLIVE by clicking the filter button (Figure 5).
  1. Then, click the Open folder in the row of the PODTLIVE parameter (Figure 5).

Figure 5: Search for and Open PODTLIVE Parameter

 

  1. The parameter PODTLIVE is used to set the age (in years) of purchase orders to be archived. In this example, the value for this parameter could be set to “1” for one year (Figure 6).
  1. If setting or editing this parameter, click the floppy disk to Save changes (Figure 6).

Figure 6: Set PODTLIVE Parameter
